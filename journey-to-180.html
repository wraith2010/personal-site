On the math. BMI is exact: (weight × 703) ÷ height². Body fat % uses the U.S. Navy formula: 86.010 × log₁₀(waist − neck) − 70.041 × log₁₀(height) + 36.76. Body fat values for weights other than the one you measure assume waist and neck shrink proportionally to weight lost (about 1 inch off the waist per 10 lb, 0.2 inch off the neck) — a useful approximation, but the real curve depends on the fat-to-muscle ratio of weight you lose.

On the timeline. Projections assume steady loss at 1.5 or 2 lb per week. Reality is rarely linear — expect a faster first month (water weight), slower middle phases, occasional plateaus, and diminishing returns near goal as your maintenance calories drop. Resistance training plus 0.7–1 g of protein per pound of body weight protects lean mass through the deficit. This page persists your last entered values locally; nothing leaves your device.
